Overview

FALCON (Forced Alignment through Contrastive Optimization Networks) is a fully differentiable neural forced aligner. A convolutional acoustic encoder, a bidirectional-LSTM phoneme classifier, and a Soft Dynamic Programming decoder are trained jointly, so gradients flow through the entire alignment.

The encoder is trained with a margin-based contrastive loss whose hard negatives are sampled at phoneme boundaries; a learnable weight balances acoustic boundary sharpness against phoneme-posterior consistency inside the Soft-DP decoder. The result is precise phoneme- and word-level timestamps that are competitive with or surpass MFA, MMS, and WhisperX on read (TIMIT) and spontaneous (Buckeye) English — and generalize zero-shot to unseen languages by mapping any phoneme inventory into a shared 39-phone space via articulatory-feature distances.
